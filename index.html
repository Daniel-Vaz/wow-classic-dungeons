    Two map styles, two eras

    Original Classic WoW shipped with no in-game dungeon maps. The community filled that gap — the Atlas Classic addon hand-drew every dungeon and labelled its bosses and points of interest. Those are the Atlas Classic maps shown here by default.

    Cataclysm finally added official in-game maps — but the expansion also physically reworked many of these dungeons, so the modern map art often no longer matches the original Classic-era layout, bosses, or routes. We keep both so you can pick the one true to your game version.

    Dungeons notably changed in Cataclysm:

    • Sunken Temple — heavily cut down; only the middle floor remains accessible.
    • Maraudon — split into three separate instances (Wicked Grotto, Foulspore Cavern, Earth Song Falls).
    • Stratholme — Live and Undead sides split into two instances (Main Gate / Service Entrance).
    • Wailing Caverns — the Winding Chasm section was removed and its bosses relocated.
    • Deadmines, Shadowfang Keep, The Stockade — layouts largely intact, but mobs and bosses were replaced to fit the new Cataclysm storylines.
